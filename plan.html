Why the plan isn't "eat less"

A normal BMI can still hide fat around the liver and organs ("lean NAFLD"). In South Asians this shows up at lower weights, which is why liver, sugar and cholesterol flag even though the scale looks fine. The fix isn't fewer calories — it's swapping refined carbs/sugar for protein and fibre, moving far more across the day, and adding light strength work. Track waist (<90 cm) and lab numbers, not the scale — and don't lose weight on purpose; keep the muscle and gain a little.

The 6-month goal, in four outcomes

① HbA1c under 5.7% — out of the pre-diabetic band  ·  ② LDL toward <100, HDL ≥55, triglycerides <90, hs-CRP low-risk  ·  ③ ALT normal, fatty-liver grade easing, Vitamin D ≥40  ·  ④ waist <90 cm and blood counts confirmed normal on a hydrated, smoke-free recheck.

How to use this

Weeks stack into months, months into the goal

Don't do everything on day one. Each week adds one or two sticky changes on top of the last.

Weekly

2–4 concrete actions tagged Eat, Move, Habit or Test. Add them; keep last week's.

Monthly

Each month targets one system with a goal and a "win condition" to check against.

6 months

Two re-test checkpoints (Month 3 & 6) show the numbers moving toward the four outcomes.

Constants that run all 26 weeks: no smoking · no alcohol · ~2.5–3 L water daily · prescribed Vitamin D and liver/bone medication · 7–8 h sleep · a 10-minute walk after meals. Everything below is on top of these.

The plan

Month by month, week by week

Month 1 · Cut the sugar spikes & start moving

Weeks 1–4 · the foundation
Monthly goal: remove the biggest blood-sugar drivers, build the post-meal-walk habit, and put a baseline on paper (waist, weight, a few days' food).
W1Measure & cut liquid sugar
  • EatDrop all sugary drinks & packaged juice. Switch to water, buttermilk, or no-sugar nimbu pani. Chai/coffee to ½ the usual sugar.
  • Move10-min walk after lunch and dinner — even indoors.
  • HabitNote weight + waist at the navel and photograph 3 typical days' meals — the honest baseline.
W2Fix the plate order
  • EatProtein + veg/salad first, carbs last — slows the sugar release.
  • EatHalve the white rice at one meal; fill with dal + sabzi + salad.
  • MoveA 5-min movement snack every hour at the desk — stand, stretch, refill water.
W3Upgrade snacks & breakfast
  • EatSwap evening biscuits/namkeen for roasted chana, makhana, fruit + nuts, or sprouts.
  • EatProtein breakfast: eggs / moong-dal chilla / curd + oats — not just toast or paratha.
  • HabitA 1-litre bottle on the desk, refilled twice before evening.
W4Lock it & lengthen walks
  • MoveGrow one daily walk to 20–25 minutes, brisk.
  • EatChai/coffee now minimal/no sugar; try unsweetened black coffee (liver-friendly).
  • HabitRe-measure; remove the easy junk from the kitchen.
Win condition: zero sugary drinks, two post-meal walks most days, baseline waist written down.

Month 2 · Build the engine — cardio + first strength

Weeks 5–8 · activity & cholesterol
Monthly goal: reach ~150 min/week brisk activity, start twice-weekly strength, add fibre/fat swaps that pull LDL and triglycerides down.
W5Set a step floor
  • MoveAim 6,000 steps/day — walking calls + a longer evening walk.
  • EatSoluble fibre daily: oats or a spoon of psyllium (isabgol) — lowers LDL.
  • HabitFix an evening walk time and protect it like a meeting.
W6Start strength (beginner)
  • Move2 short sessions: bodyweight squats, knee push-ups, glute bridges, a plank — 20 min. Muscle is a glucose "sink" and protects bone.
  • EatProtein at every meal to support training.
  • MoveStep floor up to 7,000/day.
W7Swap the fats
  • EatDeep-fried → once a week. Cook with mustard/olive oil; bake/roast/air-fry.
  • EatA daily handful of nuts + flax/chia — good fats for LDL and liver.
  • MoveAdd a 3rd strength session or a weekend hike.
W8Tame outside food
  • EatOutside/junk → max twice a week. Out: roti over naan, tandoori/grilled over creamy/fried, add salad, skip the sweet & soft drink.
  • HabitSunday meal-prep: boil eggs, cook a dal/rajma batch, chop veg.
  • MoveStep floor 8,000/day; ~150 active min for the week.
Win condition: ~150 active min/week, 2–3 strength sessions, fibre daily, outside food ≤ twice/week.

Month 3 · Hold the line & re-test

Weeks 9–12 · first checkpoint
Monthly goal: keep Months 1–2 on autopilot, deepen consistency, and re-test (hydrated, smoke-free) to see the numbers move.
W9Consistency over intensity
  • HabitA daily tick-list: walks ✓ steps ✓ no sugary drink ✓ veg+protein ✓. Aim 6/7 days.
  • MoveHold 8,000 steps + 2–3 strength; add a little load (more reps or a backpack on walks).
W10Sharpen carbs
  • EatShift one daily grain to whole/low-GI: oats, whole-wheat roti, brown rice, or millets (ragi/bajra — also bone-friendly).
  • EatKeep fruit whole, never juiced.
W11Prep for the re-test
  • HabitHydrate well the evening before and morning of (still fast) so blood counts read true.
  • TestBook: CBC, ferritin, HbA1c, fasting lipid, Vitamin D. Ask about fasting insulin/HOMA-IR.
W12Review & recalibrate
  • TestWith the doctor: Hb/HCT settling? Vitamin D past 30? LDL responding, or medication now? Ask about a DEXA bone scan.
  • HabitAdjust the next 90 days; celebrate the wins.
Checkpoint: bring both reports + the new one so the doctor sees the trend, not a snapshot.

Month 4 · Deepen the metabolic gains

Weeks 13–16 · liver & insulin sensitivity
Monthly goal: push activity and food quality a notch to keep shrinking liver fat and improve sugar handling — without losing weight.
W13Add zone-2 cardio
  • MoveTwo 30–40 min steady walks/cycles/hikes where you can still talk — great for liver fat & insulin sensitivity.
  • EatSweets/mithai become a once-a-week treat, eaten after a meal.
W14Time your eating
  • EatFinish dinner ~3 h before bed, keep it the lightest meal.
  • HabitNo screens 30 min before bed; aim 7.5–8 h sleep.
W15Progress strength
  • MoveStrength 3×/week and harder: full push-ups, split squats, band rows, longer planks.
  • EatSpread protein (~25–30 g/meal): eggs, dal+curd, paneer, chicken, fish.
W16Audit & tidy
  • HabitRe-check waist & tick-list. Fix the one slip (late snacks? weekend outside food?).
  • MoveHold 8,000–10,000 steps; sit < 30 min at a stretch.
Win condition: 3 strength + 2 zone-2 sessions/week, dinner 3 h before bed, waist holding/shrinking.

Month 5 · Strength, bone & cholesterol

Weeks 17–20 · protect the skeleton
Monthly goal: get Vitamin D to target territory, push resistance training for bone, and squeeze the last bit out of cholesterol with diet.
W17Bone-load training
  • MoveWeight-bearing + resistance 3×/week (squats, lunges, step-ups, uphill). Bone responds to load — key with osteoporosis history.
  • EatCalcium daily: curd, paneer, milk, ragi, sesame, greens — paired with Vitamin D.
W18Confirm Vitamin D plan
  • TestCheck D is heading ≥30–40 and a maintenance dose is set for after repletion.
  • Habit15–20 min midday sun most days.
W19Cholesterol fine-tuning
  • EatLock in oats/psyllium + nuts + beans daily; minimise butter/ghee/fried. If LDL is still high, revisit medication.
  • EatOmega-3 2–3×/week (fish, or walnuts/flax).
W20Make it identity
  • HabitThese are now defaults, not a "diet". Chai tastes fine with little sugar; walks feel normal.
  • MoveTry a new active thing you enjoy (longer hike, a sport).
Win condition: resistance 3×/week, calcium + sun daily, Vitamin D plan confirmed, fibre/omega-3 routine.

Month 6 · Full re-panel & the scorecard

Weeks 21–26 · the finish line
Monthly goal: consolidate every habit, take the complete panel, lay it against the four outcomes, then set the next plan.
W21–22Peak consistency
  • HabitRun the full routine clean — walks, steps, strength, plate order, no sugary drinks, sleep.
  • HabitRe-measure: waist down, weight steady = muscle gained, fat lost.
W23–24Full re-test
  • TestRepeat the complete panel (hydrated, fasting). Consider a liver ultrasound/FibroScan and ApoB for sharper heart-risk.
  • TestCheck resting blood pressure — it wasn't in the panels and is worth knowing.

  • TestCompare to the four outcomes; mark what's done, what to maintain, what still needs work.

Finish line: expected by now — sugar under the line, cholesterol improving, Vitamin D healthy, liver protected, blood counts confirmed.

Diet playbook

What to eat — the detail

One frame for every plate: ½ vegetables/salad, ¼ protein, ¼ smart carb, eaten in that order.

Build meals on these

  • Protein (every meal): eggs, dal, rajma, chana, paneer, tofu, curd, chicken, fish.
  • Fibre & veg: salad or sabzi at every meal; sprouts; non-starchy veg.
  • Smart carbs (smaller): whole-wheat roti, oats, brown rice, millets (ragi, bajra).
  • Good fats: nuts, seeds (flax, chia, pumpkin), mustard/olive oil, fish.
  • Whole fruit: apple, guava, papaya, berries, orange — never juiced.
  • Bone allies: curd, paneer, milk, ragi, sesame, leafy greens.
  • Liver allies: unsweetened black coffee, green tea, plenty of veg, omega-3.

Keep these rare

  • Liquid sugar: soft drinks, packaged juice, sweetened chai — the #1 cut.
  • Sweets & bakery: mithai, biscuits, cakes, chocolates.
  • Refined carbs: white bread/maida, big white-rice portions, instant noodles.
  • Fried & fast food: samosa, pakora, fries, chips, namkeen.
  • Creamy gravies (butter/korma), excess ghee/butter.
  • Processed snacks with added sugar or palm oil — read the label.
  • Alcohol — already stopped; keep it that way for the liver.

Lifestyle playbook

Beating the desk-and-evening slump

A sedentary day is the biggest non-diet lever. The goal isn't one big workout — it's moving little and often, all day.

At work (the sitting problem)

  • Don't sit > 30 min — stand, stretch or walk 2–3 min. Hourly phone nudge.
  • Walk phone calls; take stairs by default.
  • A water refill = a movement break. Keep the bottle just out of reach.
  • Stand for emails; a few squats/calf-raises by the desk count.
  • Target 8,000–10,000 steps/day — mostly micro-walks + post-meal walks.

Evenings & routine

  • Fixed evening walk (20–30 min) — the main daily cardio.
  • Strength 2–3×/week at home — bodyweight → bands/weights.
  • Wind-down: dinner ~3 h before bed, screens off 30 min prior, 7.5–8 h sleep.
  • Midday sun 15–20 min for Vitamin D and mood.
  • Stress: cortisol already normalised — keep it there with sleep, walks, slow breathing.
  • Guardrails: stay smoke-free and alcohol-free — they protect the liver & blood-count wins.
E

What else can be done?

Extra levers worth considering

Beyond diet and movement — useful tests, simple tools, and setup. Anything clinical goes through the doctor.

Tests worth discussing

  • Fasting insulin + HOMA-IR — confirms the insulin resistance behind lean fatty liver & the HbA1c creep.
  • Ferritin — to read the high iron saturation correctly before any iron supplement.
  • ApoB and/or Lp(a) once — a sharper heart-risk picture than LDL alone.
  • Liver ultrasound / FibroScan at ~6–12 months — to see if the grade is easing.
  • DEXA bone-density scan — given the osteoporosis history.
  • Repeat thyroid (TSH) — sits upper-normal; worth a routine recheck.

Simple tools & tracking

  • A tape measure — weekly waist beats the scale here.
  • Home BP monitor — blood pressure wasn't in the panels; cheap and important.
  • Step counter (the phone) — what's measured gets walked.
  • A 2-week CGM trial (if the doctor's open) — shows which meals spike you.
  • A habit-streak tracker — walks, steps, no-sugar-drink, protein+veg.

Supplements (ask the doctor)

  • Vitamin D — prescribed repletion + maintenance (priority, for bones).
  • Omega-3 — for triglycerides and liver fat if diet isn't enough.
  • Psyllium (isabgol) — cheap soluble fibre that lowers LDL.
  • Creatine monohydrate — supports muscle & bone alongside training (discuss).
  • Hold off on iron and random multivitamins until ferritin is checked.

Set up the environment

  • Stock healthy defaults: eggs, curd, dal, oats, fruit, nuts, frozen veg.
  • Keep junk out of the house — can't snack on what isn't there.
  • Batch-cook on a weekend for low-effort weekday meals.
  • An accountability nudge — a walking buddy or a monthly self-review.
  • Plan outside meals rather than defaulting to them.

The whole plan in one line

Move little-and-often all day, eat protein + fibre before carbs, cut liquid sugar and fried food, add light strength for muscle and bone, sleep well — and let the December re-test prove it. Already a healthy weight; this is about what's happening inside.
